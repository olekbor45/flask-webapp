FAS

FREE ALONGSIDE SHIP

Free Alongside Ship (FAS) is an Incoterm used exclusively for sea or inland waterway transport, where the seller delivers the goods alongside the vessel at a named port (e.g., on a quay or barge), and the buyer takes over from that point.

Key Points:

1.Seller is responsible for export clearance and delivery up to the shipâ€™s side at the port.

2.Risk and cost transfer to the buyer once goods are placed alongside the vessel.

3.Buyer handles loading onto the ship, main transport, insurance, and all import formalities.

4.Commonly used for bulk or non-containerized cargo.
